Education Required


Image from Carolroth

To be a pharmacist you must have a Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D.) degree which is a postgraduate professional degree, obtained from Doctor of Pharmacy programs.

To be accepted into a Pharm.D. program, you must first complete a pre-professional class which is two years and includes courses in biology, chemistry, physics, anatomy and physiology.

Doctor of Pharmacy programs take 4 years to finish, although some are 3 years. A Pharm.D. program includes courses in chemistry, pharmacology, and medical ethics.
